CHAYA Initiative

Climate Health Adaptation for Workers

A multidisciplinary research initiative integrating Artificial Intelligence, Digital Twins, IoT, Occupational Health, and Climate Science to pioneer heat-illness prediction and prevention.

Explore Research

Scientific Objectives

Developing an intelligent occupational health surveillance ecosystem through multimodal data acquisition and predictive modeling.

Climate & Environmental Science

Advanced assessment of workplace heat exposure and dynamic environmental stressors utilizing highly sensitive sensory arrays.

Artificial Intelligence

Implementation of advanced machine learning algorithms for real-time heat illness risk prediction.

Digital Twin Framework

Instantiation of accurate virtual representations combining physiological and environmental telemetry.

Occupational Medicine

Formulation of evidence-based preventive interventions adhering to strict physiological safety guidelines.

System Architecture & Simulation

A rigorous demonstration of the telemetry pipeline and Digital Twin visualization.

Data Acquisition
Integration of wearable biomedical sensors and environmental IoT on physical subjects.
Edge Telemetry
Secure, high-frequency data transmission utilizing microcontrollers to central cloud repositories.
AI/ML Engine
Processing multidimensional arrays through predictive models to compute real-time illness probability.
Digital Twin Dashboard
Visualization of physiological states and environmental overlays for immediate remedial occupational interventions.
National Implementation Framework
Strategic deployment across 4 critical regions of India: North, South, East, and West to capture diverse climactic and occupational stress patterns.

Research Consortium

A multidisciplinary coalition of specialists across occupational health, artificial intelligence, and epidemiological sciences.
